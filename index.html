Why Model NASS Exists

Following years of youth-led protests and national upheaval, the Federal Republic of Mona established the Model National Assembly — a youth deliberative body empowered to debate national crises and submit resolutions directly to the National Assembly and the President.

Model NASS is not symbolic. It is consultative, urgent, and consequential. Its sessions mirror real legislative pressure, ethical tension, and national responsibility.

The Boundary Crisis

A long-disputed border between two states, two peoples, and two faiths has erupted once again — this time over land rich in coltan, a mineral critical to global technology.

As violence spreads and displacement grows, the Federal Government has invoked emergency powers and summoned the Model National Assembly to deliberate.

Delegates must confront questions of justice, federal authority, economic survival, and coexistence — under pressure, in real time.

Who Can Apply?
