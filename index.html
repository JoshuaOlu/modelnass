Model National Assembly

A youth legislative assembly created in the aftermath of national unrest — where young delegates deliberate, negotiate, and shape the future of the Republic of Mona.

Why Model NASS Exists

Years ago, a nationwide youth revolt shook Mona. What began as peaceful protest escalated into violent confrontation after state suppression. When the dust settled, one truth was undeniable: the youth could no longer remain spectators in national affairs.

In response, the National Assembly established the Model National Assembly (Model NASS) — a formal youth deliberative body empowered to debate national issues and submit fast-tracked resolutions directly to the National Assembly and the President.

Emergency Session: The Boundary Proposal

A disputed strip of land lies between two states. Beneath it lies a rare mineral critical to Mona’s economic future.

For decades, the border communities have lived with unresolved claims, fragile coexistence, and buried resentment. Now, a foreign mining investment has brought those tensions to the surface.

Regional guards are mobilizing. Communities are fleeing. The Federal Government has declared a National Emergency.

The Model National Assembly has been summoned to deliberate — not on a bill, but on a Resolution for Action: a proposal that could stabilize the region… or deepen the fracture.

Who Should Apply

This simulation is open to young people interested in governance, public policy, law, political science, conflict resolution, research, and nation-building.

Only 36 delegates will be selected to represent diverse interests within the Assembly.
