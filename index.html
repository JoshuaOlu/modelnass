What Is the Federal Republic of Mona?

The Federal Republic of Mona is a fictional nation created solely for the Model NASS simulation. It provides a neutral but realistic setting where participants can explore governance, law, conflict, and policy without the constraints of real-world politics.

While the country is fictional, the problems, decisions, and pressures are intentionally real.

Why Model NASS Exists

Five years ago, Mona experienced a nationwide youth movement demanding inclusion in governance. In response, the Civic Inclusion and Participatory Governance Reform Act established Model NASS: a formal youth legislative body designed to debate national issues and submit advisory resolutions to the President and National Assembly.

Model NASS exists to test ideas, challenge assumptions, and prepare young leaders to think like lawmakers.

The Tarsah-Ilen Boundary Crisis

Along the border of Darah State and Ilen State lies the Tarsah-Ilen Plains, a territory long disputed over ancestry, identity, and faith.

The recent discovery of massive coltan deposits, and a proposed foreign investment deal, has reignited tensions. Violence has erupted, lives have been lost, and displacement is growing.

In response, the President has formally requested an Emergency Session of Model NASS to draft a youth-driven policy resolution.

Who Is This For?

Students and young professionals interested in governance, law, policy, research, leadership, and civic engagement.

No prior simulation experience is required. Only seriousness, curiosity, and a willingness to think deeply.
